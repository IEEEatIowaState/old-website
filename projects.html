Projects

Students involved in IEEE have the opportunity to apply what they learn in the classroom by participating in a project group. Students can choose a project that is of interest to them. Each project is sponsored by IEEE so the parts and equipment is paid for by the club. When the projects are completed the students have opportunities to demonstrate the project at different events at Iowa State.

Have a suggestion for a project idea? Email one of the IEEE Project Co-Chairs! The current project co-chairs can be found by navigating to About Us > Cabinet. In the email, please include the project idea, a short project description, and any other pertinent information.

TV-B-Gone

Inspired by Mitch Altman's original TV-B-Gone. Students learned how to design a printed circuit in Kicad. The final remote could turn off most Tv's within 20 feet.

Color Organ

This nifty device uses butterworth bandpass filters with center frequencies at 100Hz, 1.3kHz, and 3kHz. When plugged in to your favorite jam the leds will flash to the music.
Surpringsly enough, this concept has been around since the 1500's and was finally patented in 1877 for the pipe organ.

Tesla Coil Speaker

With a high quality audio input source, the tesla coil can become a speaker. Operating at a voltage around 50kV, a plasma flame is created that arcs into the world. The circuitry allows the spark to turn on and off extremely fast, this is modulated with the Audio signal, and the overall effect is a sound emanating from the high voltage arc.

Ghost Drive

A Raspberry Pi serves as the brains of the Ghost Drive. The Pi runs a custom distribution of Linux which provides a Web based GUI for accessing pictures, music and videos hosted on the Pi. The whole system is housed inside a lunch box with a small Wi-Fi router and a rechargeable battery. The portability and anonymity of the setup allows it to be used in a variety of applications.

Rubik's Cube Solver

We are working on a 3-armed robot capable of rotating a Rubik's Cube; each arm uses two motors, hence the need for a second NXT kit. Over the robot we plan to mount an Android phone and use its camera to read the current state of the Rubik's Cube. This data will be entered into an algorithm, which will produce commands to send back to the robot in order to solve the cube.

Coilgun

One project currently in progress is the creation of a coilgun. To the right the overall background behind coilguns can be seen. By sending a large amount of current through an inductor coiled around a tube, one can place a magnetic projectile in the tube to see it "launch" upon a reaction of current. As of right now the project is functional but still needs to be completed, and an updated image of this will come as soon as it is completed.
